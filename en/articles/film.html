Directed by Morten Tyldum and written by Graham Moore, Imitation Game is based on the true story of how Turing and his team based at the Government Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park deciphered the Nazis' "unbreakable" Enigma Code during World War II and helped turn the tide of the war of the war in favor of the Allies.

But The Imitation Game is not only a war story! It is also the story of Turing, a brilliant man, who was overthrown by his own government. Indeed, in 1952, Turing was found guilty of "gross indecency" following an act of homosexuality then illegal at the time, he was forced to undergo hormonal "therapy" which was equivalent to chemical castration. He committed suicide two years later on June 7, 1954 with an apple filled with cyanide.
