“How to Make Friends”: The Ultimate Guide to Making Friends

ONLINE 04/10/2022 at 7:24 p.m.

Adrien Leveque

How to Make Friends by Dale Carnegie is a classic self-help book that has sold millions of copies and been translated into multiple languages. The book is based on Carnegie's own experiences and observations, as well as his research on the subject of human relationships.

Carnegie's central idea is that humans are social objects and the ability to make and keep friends is essential for a happy and successful life.

He begins by emphasizing the importance of friendship. He states that humans are not meant to live alone and that friends are essential to our mental and physical well-being. He goes on to explain that the ability to make friends is not an innate talent, but something that can be learned through practice and effort.

Read an excerpt from this work ↗

Next, Carnegie provides a step-by-step guide to making friends. He begins by emphasizing the importance of caring about others and making them feel important. He then advises readers to avoid negative emotions such as envy, jealousy and self-pity, as these will only serve to repel potential friends. Instead, Carnegie recommends cultivating positive emotions such as sincerity, warmth, and humor. He also emphasizes the importance of being a good listener and being caring and helpful.

Finally, Carnegie offers some tips for nurturing friendships. He suggests making an effort to stay in touch with friends, even when we're busy, and being generous with our time and attention. He also urges us to forgive when our friends make mistakes, as this is essential for a lasting friendship.

Carnegie's book is essential reading for anyone looking to improve their social skills and form lasting friendships. His advice is as relevant today as it was when the book was first published, and it will certainly help anyone who follows it.
